Crime Casino

A game coded in Python

As part of the course 15-112 (Fundementals of Programming) at Carnegie Mellon University, I made a Python game called "Crime Casino". This was created over a 5 day period and it recieved the maximum possible score of 100, from Prof. Victor Adamchik.

In this project, I created a blackjack game in which the player attempts to beat the dealer by reaching as close as possible to the score of 21 without going over.

Home Screen

Displays two options. You can either press I to go to the instructions screen or you can press R to be directed to the playing room.

Instructions Screen

Displays instructions of playing blackjack and the story behind the game This screen will only allow you to progress to the playing room by clicking R

Game Screen

BET: To begin the game you must place a bet value from the sidebar. Denominations for bets are $25,000 $50,000 $100,000 and BOUNTY which means whatever you have left in your bank. When you add an amount to your bet, it will update the current bet display.
DEAL: Once you are satisfied by the bet amount, click deal to be dealt your cards. You will notice that the bet screen disappears so no further bets can be made.
HIT: If you are not satisfied with your score and believe you can get closer to 21 without going over, press HIT to add a card to your hand. This will update your score .
STAY: Once you are satisfied with your hand, press STAY to let the Dealer, Tony and Vito play their hands.
NEW ROUND: Once the round has been played and the winners/losers decided, press new round to play the next hand.
